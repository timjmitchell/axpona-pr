## ARTICLE III — CAPITAL CONTRIBUTIONS

### 3.1 Initial Capital Contributions

(a) **General Partner.** The General Partner shall contribute $[____] to the Partnership as its initial capital contribution, representing a [____]% interest.

(b) **Limited Partner.** The Limited Partner shall contribute $[____] to the Partnership as his initial capital contribution, representing a [____]% interest.

### 3.2 Additional Capital Contributions

(a) The General Partner may, in its sole discretion, determine that additional capital contributions are required to fund the Partnership's obligations, including capital calls from the Portfolio Company.

(b) The Limited Partner shall not be obligated to make additional capital contributions beyond the initial contribution, except as expressly agreed in writing.

(c) The General Partner may make additional capital contributions on behalf of the Partnership at any time.

### 3.3 Capital Accounts

A separate capital account shall be maintained for each Partner in accordance with Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(iv). Each Partner's capital account shall be:

(a) Increased by the amount of cash and fair market value of property contributed by such Partner, and by allocations of net income and gain to such Partner;

(b) Decreased by the amount of cash and fair market value of property distributed to such Partner, and by allocations of net losses and deductions to such Partner.

### 3.4 No Interest on Capital

No Partner shall be entitled to receive interest on any capital contribution or capital account balance.

### 3.5 Return of Capital

No Partner shall have the right to withdraw or demand the return of all or any portion of such Partner's capital contribution, except upon dissolution or as otherwise provided in this Agreement.

## ARTICLE IV — ALLOCATIONS AND DISTRIBUTIONS

### 4.1 Allocation of Profits and Losses

(a) **Net Profits.** Net profits of the Partnership for each fiscal year shall be allocated ninety-nine percent (99%) to the Limited Partner and one percent (1%) to the General Partner, or in such other proportions as the General Partner may determine, consistent with the Partners' economic arrangement.

(b) **Net Losses.** Net losses of the Partnership for each fiscal year shall be allocated in the same proportions as net profits.

(c) **Regulatory Allocations.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, the following special allocations shall be made to the extent required by the Treasury Regulations:
- (i) **Qualified Income Offset** (Treas. Reg. § 1.704-1(b)(2)(ii)(d));
- (ii) **Minimum Gain Chargeback** (Treas. Reg. § 1.704-2(f));
- (iii) **Partner Nonrecourse Debt Minimum Gain Chargeback** (Treas. Reg. § 1.704-2(i)(4));
- (iv) **Nonrecourse Deductions** allocated in accordance with the Partners' interests in the Partnership.

(d) **Section 704(c) Allocations.** To the extent required by Section 704(c) of the Internal Revenue Code, income, gain, loss, and deduction with respect to any property contributed to the Partnership shall be allocated so as to take into account any variation between the adjusted basis of such property and its fair market value at the time of contribution.

### 4.2 Distributions

(a) **Timing.** The General Partner shall distribute available cash to the Partners as soon as practicable after receipt of distributions from the Portfolio Company, after setting aside reasonable reserves for Partnership expenses and obligations.

(b) **Priority.** Distributions shall be made in the following order:
- (i) First, to pay or reserve for Partnership expenses, taxes, and obligations;
- (ii) Second, to the Partners in proportion to their respective percentage interests.

(c) **Tax Distributions.** If the Partnership has taxable income but insufficient cash to distribute, the General Partner shall use reasonable efforts to cause the Portfolio Company to make tax distributions sufficient to cover the Partners' estimated tax liabilities.

### 4.3 Withholding

The Partnership shall withhold from distributions any amounts required by applicable tax law. Any amounts so withheld shall be treated as having been distributed to the affected Partner.

## ARTICLE V — MANAGEMENT

### 5.1 Management by General Partner

The General Partner shall have exclusive authority over and responsibility for the management, operation, and control of the Partnership and its business. The General Partner shall manage the Partnership in its capacity as General Partner and not as an agent of the Limited Partner.

### 5.2 Powers of General Partner

Without limiting the generality of Section 5.1, the General Partner shall have the power and authority to:

(a) Execute documents and agreements on behalf of the Partnership, including the Portfolio Company's operating agreement or shareholders' agreement;

(b) Make capital contributions to the Portfolio Company and accept capital calls;

(c) Vote or consent to matters submitted to the members or shareholders of the Portfolio Company;

(d) Receive and distribute cash and property from the Portfolio Company;

(e) Negotiate and execute the sale, transfer, or other disposition of the Partnership's interest in the Portfolio Company;

(f) Open and maintain bank accounts;

(g) Engage attorneys, accountants, and other professional advisors;

(h) File tax returns and make tax elections on behalf of the Partnership;

(i) Take all actions necessary or appropriate to carry out the purposes of the Partnership.

### 5.3 Delegation

The General Partner may delegate any of its powers to agents, employees, or professional advisors, provided that the General Partner shall remain responsible for the actions of any such delegate.

### 5.4 Limitation on Limited Partner Authority

The Limited Partner shall not participate in the management or control of the Partnership's business and shall have no power or authority to act for or bind the Partnership. Nothing herein shall be construed to limit the Limited Partner's rights as an investor or his ability to exercise the rights set forth in this Agreement.

### 5.5 Exculpation and Indemnification

(a) **Standard of Care.** The General Partner shall not be liable to the Partnership or the Limited Partner for any act or omission taken in good faith and in a manner reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Partnership, except in the case of gross negligence, willful misconduct, bad faith, or fraud.

(b) **Indemnification.** The Partnership shall indemnify and hold harmless the General Partner and its members, managers, and agents from and against any losses, damages, claims, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising from any act or omission in connection with the Partnership's business, except to the extent arising from gross negligence, willful misconduct, bad faith, or fraud.

### 6.1 Books and Records

The General Partner shall maintain complete and accurate books and records of the Partnership at the Partnership's principal office. The Limited Partner shall have the right to inspect and copy such books and records during reasonable business hours upon reasonable notice, as provided by 6 Del. C. § 17-305.

### 6.2 Fiscal Year

The fiscal year of the Partnership shall be the calendar year.

### 6.3 Tax Returns

The General Partner shall cause the Partnership to prepare and file all required federal, state, and local tax returns, including IRS Form 1065 (to the extent required — see Section 6.5). The General Partner shall furnish each Partner with a Schedule K-1 on or before March 15 of each year (or such later date as may be permitted by applicable extensions).

### 6.4 Partnership Representative

The General Partner is hereby designated as the **"Partnership Representative"** within the meaning of Section 6223 of the Internal Revenue Code (as amended by the Bipartisan Budget Act of 2015). The Partnership Representative shall have all powers and authority granted by the Code and Treasury Regulations.

### 6.5 Election Out of BBA Audit Regime

The Partnership Representative shall elect out of the centralized partnership audit regime pursuant to Section 6221(b) of the Internal Revenue Code for each taxable year for which the Partnership is eligible to make such election.

### 6.6 Tax Classification

The Partners acknowledge that the Partnership may be treated as a disregarded entity for federal income tax purposes if the GP LLC is treated as a disregarded entity (single-member LLC) and Henry is the sole LP. In such case, the Partnership's income and deductions would be reported directly on Henry's personal tax return (Form 1040) without the need for a separate Form 1065. The General Partner shall consult with tax counsel to determine the appropriate tax reporting treatment and shall report consistently with such determination.

### 6.7 Tax Elections

The General Partner may make any tax election available to the Partnership under the Internal Revenue Code or applicable state tax law, including but not limited to elections under Sections 754, 704(c), and 761 of the Code.

## ARTICLE VII — TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

### 7.1 Restrictions on Transfer

No Partner may sell, assign, transfer, pledge, or otherwise dispose of all or any portion of its interest in the Partnership without the prior written consent of the General Partner.

### 7.2 Permitted Transfers

Notwithstanding Section 7.1, the Limited Partner may transfer all or any portion of his interest to:

(a) A trust for the benefit of the Limited Partner or his immediate family members;

(b) The Limited Partner's spouse, children, or other lineal descendants;

(c) An entity wholly owned by the Limited Partner or the foregoing persons;

provided that any such transferee agrees in writing to be bound by the terms of this Agreement and no such transfer shall cause the Partnership to be treated as a publicly traded partnership.

### 7.3 Effect of Prohibited Transfer

Any purported transfer in violation of this Article VII shall be void and of no effect.

## ARTICLE VIII — DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

### 8.1 Events of Dissolution

The Partnership shall be dissolved upon the earliest to occur of:

(a) The written election of the General Partner to dissolve the Partnership;

(b) The sale or other disposition of all of the Partnership's interest in the Portfolio Company and the distribution of all proceeds therefrom;

(c) The death, disability, bankruptcy, or dissolution of the General Partner, unless within ninety (90) days the Limited Partner elects in writing to continue the Partnership and appoints a successor General Partner;

(d) Entry of a judicial decree of dissolution under 6 Del. C. § 17-802.

### 8.2 Winding Up

Upon dissolution, the General Partner (or a liquidating trustee, if the General Partner is unable to serve) shall wind up the affairs of the Partnership and liquidate its assets. The proceeds shall be applied in the following order:

(a) First, to pay or provide for the debts and obligations of the Partnership, including expenses of liquidation;

(b) Second, to establish any reserves that the General Partner deems reasonably necessary for contingent or unforeseen liabilities;

(c) Third, to the Partners in proportion to and to the extent of their positive capital account balances.

### 8.3 Final Distributions

Liquidating distributions shall be made within ninety (90) days after dissolution (or as soon as practicable thereafter) in accordance with positive capital account balances, as required by Treasury Regulation Section 1.704-1(b)(2)(ii)(b).

### 8.4 Cancellation of Certificate

Upon completion of winding up and final distribution, the General Partner shall file a Certificate of Cancellation of the Partnership with the Secretary of State of the State of Delaware.

## ARTICLE IX — KEY PERSON PROVISIONS

### 9.1 Key Person Event

A **"Key Person Event"** shall be deemed to have occurred if Henry [Last Name]:

(a) Dies;

(b) Becomes permanently disabled (unable to manage Partnership affairs for a period of one hundred eighty (180) consecutive days);

(c) Is convicted of a felony involving fraud, dishonesty, or moral turpitude; or

(d) Files for personal bankruptcy.

### 9.2 Consequences of Key Person Event

Upon a Key Person Event:

(a) The General Partner's authority shall be suspended pending resolution;

(b) If Henry has designated a successor manager for the GP LLC in his estate planning documents or operating agreement, such successor shall assume control;

(c) If no successor is designated, the Partnership shall be dissolved in accordance with Article VIII;

(d) The Partnership's interest in the Portfolio Company shall continue to be held (and its economic rights preserved) pending orderly wind-down.
