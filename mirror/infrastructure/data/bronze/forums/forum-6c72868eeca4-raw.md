Relatively few rooms that made me want to stay for track after track. 
The biggest surprise room for me was the Sonner room (719). Best imaging of the show, no contest. The owner(?) was over the moon giddy/euphoric about the sound (as he should be...knocked it out of the park). Somehow found that perfect millimeter placement where the room was just singing. Worth seeking out 
In general, I found myself gravitating more to the analog rooms on this visit. Very much a surprise for me, since historically I've been much more engaged with the digital rooms and tended to bypass the analog rooms. As I mentioned to Chris and some others at the show, I blame this on my recent experience with the Taiko Olympus. I'm just gravitating more to what the best analog brings to music, and have less patience for typical digital. 
The Refined Audio room (688) was a fantastic blend of both. The analog was truly outstanding, and you could get lost in it. The digital (Taiko Extreme through the Audio Mirror DAC) was similarly engaging, but in a different way. Both invited you to relax and just enjoy the music for a while, which was more the exception than the rule at the show (show conditions are so tough)
