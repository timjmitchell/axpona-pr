## 1. Delaware LLC Formation for Acquiring Entity

### Formation Requirements

**Certificate of Formation:** File with the Delaware Division of Corporations. Requires only LLC name, registered agent information, and organizer details. Filing fee: **$110**.

**Operating Agreement:** Not filed with the state but effectively required. Should cover member rights, management structure, capital contributions, profit/loss allocation, and transfer restrictions. Delaware law gives maximum flexibility to customize.

**Registered Agent:** Must designate a registered agent with a physical street address in Delaware. Professional services cost **$50-$300/year**.

### Timeline and Costs

| Item | Cost | Timeline |
|------|------|----------|
| Certificate of Formation | $110 | 10-15 business days (standard) |
| Same-day expedited filing | $110 + $1,000 | Same day |
| 24-hour expedited filing | $110 + $100-$500 | 1 business day |
| Registered agent (annual) | $50-$300 | Ongoing |
| Annual franchise tax | $300 | Due June 1 each year |
| Name reservation (optional) | $75 | — |

### Foreign Qualification Requirements

The acquiring entity must register as a foreign LLC in both Illinois and Connecticut.

**Illinois (where the event operates):**
- File "Application for Admission to Transact Business" with the Illinois Secretary of State
- Filing fee: **$150** (paper filing only)
- Must attach Certificate of Good Standing from Delaware (dated within 60 days)
- Expedited in-person service: additional $100
- Annual reporting requirements apply

**Connecticut (where the 3 employees are located):**
- File "Foreign Registration Statement" with the Connecticut Secretary of State
- Filing fee: **$120**
- Can file online, by mail, or in person
- Must provide registered agent in Connecticut
- Annual Report: **$80/year**, filed January 1–March 31

## 2. Asset Purchase Agreement — Key Legal Considerations

### Standard APA Structure

1. **Recitals** — Identify parties, describe the business, state intent
2. **Definitions** — Purchase price, assets, assumed liabilities, excluded assets/liabilities
3. **Purchase and Sale of Assets** — Detailed asset schedule
4. **Assumption of Liabilities** — Which obligations transfer to buyer
5. **Purchase Price and Payment Terms** — EBITDA-based pricing, escrow, adjustments
6. **Representations and Warranties** — Seller and buyer reps
7. **Covenants** — Pre-closing and post-closing obligations
8. **Conditions to Closing** — Conditions precedent
9. **Indemnification** — Baskets, caps, survival periods
10. **Termination** — Circumstances allowing either party to terminate
11. **Miscellaneous** — Governing law, dispute resolution, notices

### Asset Definitions

**Typically Included in Event Business Acquisitions:**
- Trade name, trademarks, and service marks (AXPONA brand)
- Copyrights (marketing materials, content, show guides)
- Domain names and websites
- Customer/exhibitor/attendee databases and CRM data
- Vendor and venue contracts (subject to assignment)
- Secured event dates with the venue
- Show history, archives, and institutional knowledge
- Goodwill and going-concern value
- Phone numbers, email addresses, social media accounts
- Prepaid expenses (to the extent of net deposits adjustment)

**Typically Excluded:**
- Cash and cash equivalents (seller keeps)
- Accounts receivable pre-closing (negotiable)
- Seller's entity-level records (tax returns, organizational docs)
- Insurance policies
- Employee benefit plan assets
- Inter-company receivables
- Personal property of seller's principals

### Purchase Price Mechanics

**EBITDA-Based Pricing:** With 2025 EBITDA of $782K and 2026 forecast of $943K, and typical event/media business multiples of **3-5x** for a business of this size (lower middle market), the deal range would be approximately **$2.3M-$4.7M**.

Note: Larger B2B event companies can command 10-13x EBITDA multiples, but those are typically larger, multi-event portfolios. A single trade show with $782K-$943K EBITDA would likely trade at a lower multiple given concentration risk.

**Payment Structure (95%/5%):**
- 95% of purchase price paid at closing
- 5% deposited in escrow for 12 months (reps/warranties/indemnification)

**Net Deposits Adjustment:** Critical mechanism for event businesses:
- **Deferred revenue** (exhibitor/sponsor payments for future events) = obligation buyer inherits
- **Prepaid expenses** (deposits on venues, services) = value buyer receives
- **Net Deposits = Deferred Revenue - Prepaid Expenses**
- If positive: purchase price adjusted downward (buyer inherits net obligation)
- If negative: purchase price adjusts upward
- Calculated as of closing date with a true-up mechanism 30-120 days post-closing

### Representations and Warranties

**Standard Seller Representations (25-40 reps):**
- Organization, authority, and enforceability
- Title to assets (free and clear of liens)
- Financial statements accuracy
- No undisclosed liabilities
- Material contracts (valid, no defaults)
- IP ownership and non-infringement
- Compliance with laws (event permits, licenses)
- Tax compliance
- Employee matters (compensation, benefits, no pending claims)
- No litigation or threatened claims
- Customer/exhibitor relationships (no notice of intent to terminate)
- Data privacy compliance (attendee/exhibitor data)
- Sufficiency of assets to operate the business
- Absence of material adverse change
- Brokers/finders fees

**Standard Buyer Representations (3-5 reps):**
- Organization and authority
- Enforceability
- No conflicts with existing obligations
- Sufficiency of funds to close
- No brokers/finders

**Fundamental Representations** (survive longer, not subject to basket/cap):
- Organization and authority
- Title to assets
- Tax matters
- No broker/finder fees

### Employee Matters

Since the 3 Connecticut employees are JDE employees (not being transferred), the buyer will extend new offer letters.

- **No successor employer obligation** in an asset purchase
- **Offer letters** should specify at-will employment, new compensation/benefits, start date at/near closing
- **WARN Act:** With only 3 employees, federal WARN (50+ threshold) and Connecticut mini-WARN (100+ threshold) likely do not apply
- **No obligation to match seller's benefits**, but competitive offers essential for key personnel
- **APA should address** whether JDE pays out accrued PTO before closing

### Contract Assignment

**Venue Contracts (Renaissance Schaumburg):**
- Hotel contracts frequently contain anti-assignment clauses
- Must obtain written consent from venue — ideally as a closing condition
- Leverage: venue contracted through 2029, commercial incentive to consent
- Consider whether venue requires financial covenants or guarantees from new entity

**Vendor Contracts:**
- Each contract must be reviewed for assignment/change-of-control provisions
- Material vendor contracts listed in APA disclosure schedules
- Consent from critical vendors should be a closing condition

**Exhibitor Contracts:**
- Existing contracts for next event create the deferred revenue being adjusted at closing
- Typically short-term (one show cycle), may not have anti-assignment language
- Best practice: notify exhibitors of ownership change
- APA should represent all exhibitor contracts are valid and enforceable

### Intellectual Property Transfer

**Trademarks:**
- File assignment with USPTO (must include goodwill — "naked assignment" without goodwill is void)
- Verify registration status and pending applications

**Copyrights:**
- Assignment must be in writing (17 U.S.C. Section 204)
- Consider registering with Copyright Office

**Domain Names:**
- Transfer registrations to buyer's account
- APA should prohibit seller from registering similar domains

**Databases:**
- Must comply with data privacy laws (CAN-SPAM, state privacy) when transferring
- APA should warrant databases collected in compliance with applicable law

### Non-Compete / Non-Solicitation

**Typical Terms for Event Industry:**
- **Duration:** 3-5 years (courts more willing to enforce in business-sale context)
- **Geographic scope:** National (trade shows draw nationally/internationally)
- **Activity scope:** Organizing, promoting, or participating as principal in competing audio/hi-fi events
- **Non-solicitation:** Prohibit soliciting exhibitors, sponsors, attendees, and 3 key employees for 3-5 years

**FTC Non-Compete Rule Status:** The FTC's 2024 rule was enjoined and appeals dismissed in September 2025. **Not in effect.** The rule always contained a carve-out for business-sale non-competes. Enforceable under state law.

### Transition Services Agreement

**Typical Terms for 1-2 Event Cycles:**
- **Duration:** 12-24 months
- **Scope:** Event planning/logistics expertise, vendor/venue introductions, exhibitor relations, knowledge transfer
- **Service levels:** Define deliverables, hours/week (tapering), response times
- **Compensation:** Usually at cost or cost-plus; may be included in purchase price
- **Termination:** Buyer can terminate individual services early
- **Liability:** Seller provides services "as-is" with limited liability

### Escrow Provisions

| Provision | Market Standard |
|-----------|----------------|
| Amount | 5% of purchase price (market range 5-15%; 5% is low end) |
| Duration | 12 months (52% of deals per SRS Acquiom data) |
| Release | Upon mutual consent or expiration if no pending claims |
| Claims | Written notice with detail; 30-day response period |
| Investment | Money market or similar; interest allocated proportionally |

### Indemnification — Market Norms ($3-6M Deal)

| Provision | Market Norm |
|-----------|-------------|
| General cap | 10-20% of purchase price ($300K-$1.2M) |
| Basket (deductible) | 0.5-1.5% of purchase price ($15K-$90K) |
| Basket type | Deductible (67% of deals) vs. tipping (33%) |
| Fundamental reps cap | Often 100% or uncapped |
| General rep survival | 12-24 months |
| Fundamental rep survival | 3-6 years or statute of limitations |
| Tax rep survival | Statute of limitations plus 60-90 days |
| Fraud | Uncapped, indefinite survival |
| De minimis threshold | $5K-$10K per claim |

### Closing Conditions

**Buyer's conditions:**
- Accuracy of seller's reps at closing
- Seller's compliance with pre-closing covenants
- No material adverse change
- Third-party consents (venue, material vendors)
- Executed IP assignment documents
- Executed bill of sale and assignment/assumption agreement
- No injunction preventing closing
- Employee offer letters accepted by key personnel

**Seller's conditions:**
- Accuracy of buyer's reps at closing
- Funds available / financing obtained
- Purchase price wire transfer
- Executed escrow agreement
- Executed assumption agreement

### Illinois Bulk Sale Considerations

**UCC Article 6:** Illinois has **repealed** its Bulk Transfers article.

**However — Illinois Revenue-Specific Obligations Still Apply:** Under Illinois Administrative Code Title 86, Section 130.1701:

- **Purchaser must notify** Illinois Department of Revenue **at least 10 business days before closing**
- Department may issue a **withholding order** within 10 business days
- Within 60 business days, Department issues final withholding amount
- Purchaser should request a **tax clearance certificate** from seller
- **Failure to comply** can make purchaser liable for seller's unpaid taxes
- **Safe harbor:** If Department fails to respond within statutory timeframes, purchaser is relieved

**Recommendation:** File bulk sale notice early. Request tax clearance certificate as a closing condition. Include seller representation that all Illinois taxes are current.

## 3. Tax Implications — Asset Purchase

### Section 1060 Allocation (Residual Method)

Both buyer and seller must allocate purchase price across seven classes. Both file **Form 8594**.

| Class | Assets | Tax Treatment for Buyer |
|-------|--------|------------------------|
| I | Cash and equivalents | No depreciation/amortization |
| II | Actively traded securities | Varies |
| III | Accounts receivable | Varies |
| IV | Inventory | Cost of goods sold |
| V | Tangible personal property | Depreciation; Section 179/bonus depreciation eligible |
| VI | Section 197 intangibles (except goodwill) — covenants not to compete, customer lists, trademarks | 15-year straight-line amortization |
| VII | Goodwill and going-concern value (residual) | 15-year straight-line amortization |

### Buyer Advantages

- **Step-up in basis** equal to allocated purchase price
- **Section 179 / Bonus Depreciation** on tangible personal property (Class V)
- **15-year amortization** of goodwill, trademarks, customer lists
- **No inherited tax liabilities** (unlike stock/equity purchase)
- **Cherry-pick assets** — exclude unwanted assets and liabilities

### Seller Implications

- **Ordinary income** on inventory, receivables, depreciation recapture
- **Capital gains** (long-term if held >1 year) on goodwill, going-concern value, appreciated capital assets
- **Covenant not to compete** payments = ordinary income
- Allocation is zero-sum: buyer wants more in depreciable classes, seller wants more in capital gains classes

### Illinois Sales/Use Tax

- Applies to tangible personal property transfers (likely minimal in this deal)
- Intangible assets (trademarks, goodwill, databases, contracts) **not subject** to Illinois sales tax

### Delaware Transfer Taxes

- Realty Transfer Tax applies only to real property — **not applicable** to this deal

## 4. Event Industry-Specific Considerations

### Venue Contract Assignment

- Renaissance Schaumburg contracts through 2029 are among the most critical assets
- **Strategy:** Engage hotel early; obtain written consent as closing condition
- Venue has commercial incentive to consent (profitable long-term tenant)
- Buyer may need to demonstrate financial capability or provide guarantees
- **Risk:** If venue refuses consent, could kill the deal or require restructuring (e.g., co-tenancy arrangement during transition)

### Exhibitor Contract Continuity

- Buyer inherits performance obligation — must deliver the event exhibitors paid for
- Send joint seller/buyer notification letter to all contracted exhibitors
- APA should represent all exhibitor contracts in good standing with no disputes

### Deferred Revenue Treatment

- **If closing before event:** Buyer inherits maximum deferred revenue and execution risk
- **If closing after event:** Net deposits adjustment is minimized
- **Tax treatment:** Under Assumption Method, deferred revenue liability is a contingent liability; costs not deductible until all-events test satisfied (i.e., event occurs)

### Goodwill Components (AXPONA-Specific)

- Brand recognition in audiophile/hi-fi community
- Event track record and venue relationship (through 2029)
- Exhibitor relationships (80%+ rebooking rate)
- Attendee database (16,000+ prospects)
- Institutional knowledge
- Assembled workforce (3 key employees)

### Key Person Risk Mitigation

- **Employment agreements** (not just at-will offers) with 1-2 year terms, non-competes, retention bonuses
- **Closing condition:** Acceptance of offer letters by key employees
- **Retention bonuses:** Vest over 12-24 months post-closing
- **TSA backstop:** Seller's transition services during initial period
- **Non-solicitation:** APA should specifically prohibit JDE from re-hiring these employees
- **Key person insurance:** Consider for most critical employees
